## 30° / 60° / 90° triangles

Angles:
* 30° or $\frac{pi}{6}$ radians
* 60° or $\frac{pi}{3}$ radians
* 90° or $\frac{pi}{2}$

When hypotenuse = 1 (ie unit circle):
    Side opposite 30° side = $\frac{1}{2}$
    Side opposite 60° side = $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$

## 45° / 45° / 90° triangles

Angles:
* 45° or $\frac{pi}{4}$ radians
* 45° or $\frac{pi}{4}$ radians
* 90° or $\frac{pi}{2}$
* When hypotenuse = 1 (ie unit circle):
    * Side opposite: $\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$
    * Side opposite: $\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$
